Robotic Science Computer
Open Science Stack (OSS) is building world’s first robotic science computer based on a general-purpose architecture:
1. A small set of stateless instructions to handle and operate off-the-shelf scientific equipment. These instructions are implemented in ROS2 (the most popular open-source framework for operating robots) to ensure that the lab can use any robot from any leading manufacturer.
2. A “universal lab” abstraction that hides away the diversity of underlying scientific equipment across physical labs.
3. A cloud-based “lab operating system” that includes I/O Engine (to handle input materials and samples), Workflow Engine (to dynamically dispatch instructions to robots & scientific equipment), Runtime Engine (to locate equipment via vision stack; to maintain runtime status of equipment; etc.), and Security Engine (to handle multiple users, ensure privacy, etc.).

Science R&D

India’s R&D spending (0.8% of the GDP) is much lower compared to other countries with similar scale and aspirations. To ensure that the R&D spends are utilized better, Open Science Stack is designing and building the specifications need to implement a general-purpose robotic science computer.

Using OSS specifications, we are building a reference implementation of “Open Science Lab”. The goal is to make cloud-based labs accessible to researchers across India and to let them run their wet lab experiments in an automated manner in a remote physical lab. The system will handle all aspects of running experiments – including handling materials (compounds, molecules, reagents, etc.), moving inputs and samples (test-tubes, well-plates, etc.), operating instruments, setting experimental parameters, waste disposal, etc. in a fully automated manner with the help of robots.

Science R&D: Drug Discovery

We are building an extensible and scalable science computer for the preclinical phases of the drug discovery phases. The initial implementation is focused on the initial phases of the drug discovery process, especially the hit identification and hit to lead phases. Open Science Lab for drug discovery will support the following capabilities:

1. Express the precise logic for each step of the drug discovery phases such as hit identification, hit to lead iterations, lead optimization, and so on.
2. Execute your programs iteratively and efficiently (24x7x365) to minimize the time required for these laborious iterative experiments of the target-to-hit, hit-to-lead, and lead optimization steps of drug discovery.
3. Get access to automated systems and robots to run your experiments more accurately and eliminate human errors that typically creep in while doing these highly repetitive experiments manually
4. Reduce the costs of doing experiments by optimizing the use of compounds / molecules and consumables (reagents, buffers, etc.) by leveraging highly-accurate automated equipment.

Education

In India,  more than 38 million high school students and 16 million senior school students study science. However, as per NCERT survey, as many as 65% schools for senior students don’t have adequate science labs -- even for the students who have opted to study science in Classes 11th and 12th! Moreover, 71% of Class 9th and 10th students in India don’t have access to adequate science labs.

Consequently, students (and teachers) resort to "learning by rote" (typically) or to "learning by example" (in the best case, when self-motivated teachers take personal initiative to make the topic more interesting). In science pedagogy, "learning by doing" is known to be more effective. To provide every student in India the opportunity to learn by doing their own experiments and, thereby, to experience the joy of science, we are building cloud-based Open Science Labs (OSL) so that they can be made available to school students.

OSL provides anytime and anywhere access to fully-equipped science labs to students. By providing opportunities to students to do science experiments with real equipment and to play with them in a controlled environment (with no risk of them breaking or getting sploit), OSL helps to strengthen students’ scientific foundations. OSL also enables teachers to fuel the spark of scientific inquiry by guiding interested students to define and run a large variety of science experiments to explore and understand the world around them. 

Partnerships
ARTPARK (AI and Robotics Research Park) is a research centre located at the Indian Institute of Science in Bangalore, India. The centre focuses on research in areas such as robotics, automation, and advanced manufacturing. In the field of robotics, IISc ARTPARK conducts research on various aspects of robotics, including control systems, artificial intelligence, machine learning, and computer vision.
Core team members: PROF BHARADWAJ AMRUTUR
Advisors: PROF MANOJ VARMA
The Systems Biology Institute (SBI) is a research institute based in Tokyo, Japan that focuses on systems biology, a field that combines biology, mathematics, and computer science to study complex biological systems. SBI has an active research program in robotics, which involves developing robots that can assist in biological research and medicine. These robots are designed to perform a range of tasks, such as handling samples, performing experiments, and analyzing data.
Core team members: SAMIK GHOSH, SUCHEE PALANIAPPAN
Advisors: HIROAKI KITANO, PROF AKIRA FUNAHASHI
About Us
Open Science Stack (OSS) is a not-for-profit initiative building a societal-scale platform for open science. OSS is building the world’s first robotic science computer using open and extensible architecture so that researchers can run any science experiment.
